Mortgage

Down Payment
An initial payment made when purchasing a house. It is a percentage of the total price that the investor pays upfront at the time of closing the deal on the property. This reduces the risk of the loan and ensures the buyer has an existing financial stake in the property.
Interest Rate
An interest rate is a measure of the cost of borrowing money from a lender to finance the purchase of a property. It is expressed a percentage and represents the additional amount the borrower must pay on top of the original loan amount as compensation to the lender for providing the funds.
Cost For Comparison Interest Rate
Cost for comparison is a standardised way of representing the total cost associated with a specific loan offer, taking into account the interest rate as well as additional fees or charges. This helps to compare different loan offers from various lenders more easily making comparisons of properties fair.
Loan Term
Refers to the length of time over which the borrower agrees to repay the mortgage loan in full. It represents the duration of the mortgage agreement and determines the number of monthly payments the borrower must make to the lender. Typically expressed in years, this can span from anywhere between 15-40 years, with 25â€“30-year mortgages being the most common at present.

Gross Yield
Gross yield is used to measure the potential return on a rental property before accounting for expenses. This is expressed as a percentage and represents the annual rental income generated by the property as a proportion of its total purchase price.
Net Yield
This is used to measure the actual return on investment of a rental property after accounting for all operating expenses and costs associated with property ownership. It represents the net income generated by the property as a percentage of its total purchase price.
Vacancy Rate
Indicates the percentage of time the property is unoccupied on an annual basis. It represents the amount of time the property will not be generated rental income for.
